— 01 / About the Lab

A research home for contact linguistics.

GlossaContact Lab investigates how languages influence each other across time and space — combining historical philology, corpus methods, and formal syntax under one roof.

Hosted by the Faculty of English Language and Literature at the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens, GlossaContact Lab brings together linguists, philologists, and digital humanists working on Greek, English, and a wide range of historical and modern contact varieties.

Our work spans several centuries and several traditions: from Byzantine and Early Modern Greek translations of biblical and classical texts, to syntactic change in the history of English, to bilingualism and language attrition in present-day communities.

The Lab works closely with international partners and welcomes Erasmus+ research interns into a tight, supportive group with regular reading sessions and hands-on training in corpus annotation.

Research Areas

  • i.Diachronic syntax of Greek & English
  • ii.Language contact & translation philology
  • iii.Treebanking & corpus annotation
  • iv.Bilingualism & heritage languages
  • v.Computational philology & digital humanities
  • vi.Voice, valency, and argument structure
— 02 / The Erasmus+ Internship

What you'll actually do here.

An internship at GlossaContact is a real research apprenticeship. You will be assigned to an active project, receive structured training, and contribute work that ends up in the lab's outputs.

i

Corpus & Treebank Work

Annotate historical Greek, Latin, or English texts; learn dependency annotation, part-of-speech tagging, and digital edition workflows alongside senior annotators.

ii

Research Project

Take on a focused mini-project — typically a case study in syntactic or lexical change — supervised by Prof. Lavidas. Work can develop into a thesis chapter or a co-authored paper.

iii

Lab Life & Seminars

Attend the Lab Seminar, the History of Linguistics reading group, and visiting-speaker series. Present your own work in progress and receive structured peer feedback.

Who can apply

Bachelor's (advanced years), Master's, and doctoral students enrolled at any Erasmus+ partner institution. Background in linguistics, philology, classics, English / Greek studies, computer science, or digital humanities is welcome. Recent graduates may apply for a traineeship within twelve months of graduation, in line with Erasmus+ rules.

Funding & logistics

The Erasmus+ traineeship grant is paid by your home institution; consult your home Erasmus office for the current monthly amount and any supplements you qualify for. The Lab assists with documentation, accommodation referrals, and welcome support — but is not the funding body.

— 03 / How to Apply

Five steps. No surprises.

Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis. We aim to give a substantive yes/no within roughly three weeks of receipt.

Step 1 · Before you write to us

Talk to your home Erasmus+ office

Confirm that you are eligible for an Erasmus+ traineeship and that your institution supports the placement. Ask about available start dates, durations, and the grant amount you would receive.

Step 2 · The package you send us

Prepare your application materials

Send (1) the completed Application Form, (2) your CV, (3) a one-page motivation letter naming a research area from §02, and (4) one academic transcript or grade summary. PDF only.

Step 3 · Submit by email

Email the package to the Lab

Send everything as a single message to Prof. Lavidas at nikolaos.lavidas@gmail.com with the subject line: "GlossaContact Erasmus+ Application — [your surname]".

Step 4 · We respond

Short interview (online)

Shortlisted candidates are invited to a brief online conversation with Prof. Lavidas to align on your interests, the lab's projects, and the practical timing of the placement.

Step 5 · Paperwork & arrival

Sign the Learning Agreement

Once accepted, we sign your Erasmus+ Learning Agreement for Traineeships and your home institution finalises the grant. We then coordinate arrival, induction, and your first project assignment.

Rolling applications

Apply at least three months before your intended start date.
